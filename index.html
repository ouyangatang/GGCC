ABOUT OUR CAMP


Go Go Girl Code Camp is a day camp for middle-school girls to introduce them to computer science, teach girls to code, and show that it can be a lot of fun!



WHO

Any girl 11 to 15 years old entering 6th, 7th, or 8th grade in the fall.

No previous programming experience necessary!

WHEN

August 1st:
9-10:00AM - Check In
4:00PM - Departure

August 2nd
9:00 AM - Arrival
4-5:00PM - Check Out

WHERE

UTDallas

ECSS Building (South Engineering and Computer Science)

Rooms 2.410 and 2.203.



There is a $15.00 tuition paid during registration to cover 2 lunches, snacks, activities, and facilities. Please note that the $15 fee is non-refundable and non-transferable.

Students must bring a laptop to be able to code at camp (and work on projects at home!). Please let us know during registration if your student needs to be provided a laptop (on loan courtesy of UTD) and one from home is not available.


ACTIVITIES

  • Animation in Alice using drag-and-drop programming
  • Lunch with cool women in the technology field
  • Introduction to web development and online courses
  • Technology based activities, games, and instruction
  • Mobile application development
  • Entrepreneurship and presentation skills with end of camp showcase


    • WHY GET GIRLS TO CODE?


    As the importance of coding as a skill continues to increase, with programming as a powerful and applicable tool to mold and utilize our very own surroundings, it seems like only one thing is left behind - girls. Girls have tremendous potential to succeed in computer science and defy the expectations set by stereotypes and industry stigma.



    26%

    Percentage of professional computing occupations in the 2013 U.S. workforce held by women.

    12%

    Percentage of 2012 computer science undergraduate degree recipients at major universities who were women, despite earning 57% of undergraduate degrees.

    64%

    Percent decline in the number of first-year undergraduate women interested in majoring in Computer Science between 2000 and 2012.

    Statistics provided by NCWIT



    Let's beat the numbers.

    MEET OUR STAFF


    Jessica Ouyang

    Founder & Director

    Go Girl Code Camp was created and organized by Jessica in efforts to share coding with more girls. She first learned to code in middle school and hopes this camp can get girls interested in STEM and computer science. She also hopes to show that computer science is not only applicable, but fun, and remove some of the stigma for girls pursuing careers in tech. This camp was created as a part of her Girl Scout Gold Award Project.
